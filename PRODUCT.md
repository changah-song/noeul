# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-studying adults learning a language (Korean, English, Chinese) through reading. They're intrinsically motivated — no teacher pushing them — and sit down with intention. They use the app in quiet moments: commute, evening wind-down, weekend study sessions. The primary task on any screen is either reading a book or reviewing vocabulary; everything else is infrastructure.

## Product Purpose

Fluent Fable makes reading in a foreign language the path of least resistance. Users load their own ebooks or pick from a public library, then read with instant dictionary lookups (tap a word), sentence-level translation (highlight a phrase), Hanja/character exploration, and spaced-repetition flashcards for the words they've saved. Success looks like a learner finishing a chapter in a target language and feeling equipped, not frustrated.

## Brand Personality

Calm, focused, literary. The app should feel like a well-lit study room — not a classroom, not a game. Confidence without coldness. The experience of reading a book you love, with a knowledgeable friend sitting nearby.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo and language-game apps**: cartoon mascots, streaks as the emotional core, bright reward loops. Fluent Fable is for readers, not gamers.
- **Anki / Quizlet**: clinical flashcard grids, no aesthetic investment. Tools that feel like homework.
- **Generic e-reader defaults (Kindle sepia)**: pleasant but anonymous. Fluent Fable has a point of view.

## Design Principles

1. **Reading is the product** — the text on screen deserves the most space and attention. UI chrome recedes; it never competes.
2. **Instant, then invisible** — lookup and translation results appear fast enough to feel like thought, then get out of the way.
3. **Earn every interruption** — no notification, badge, or gamification element that doesn't serve the learner's reading goal.
4. **Depth without clutter** — dictionary entries, Hanja breakdowns, and example sentences are rich; the UI surfaces only what's needed and hides the rest until asked.
5. **Trust the reader** — no hand-holding, no streaks, no shame. Adults who choose to read a foreign novel deserve a tool that respects their intent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text and UI components).
- Support Dynamic Type / system font scaling for readability in the reader itself.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; no animations that gate reading content.
- Character-script support: Korean (Hangul + Hanja), Chinese (Simplified + Traditional), English — glyphs and line-height must be correct for CJK text rendering.
